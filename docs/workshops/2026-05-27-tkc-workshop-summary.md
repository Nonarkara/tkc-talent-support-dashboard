## The second-AI's reconstruction (verbatim, with Non's correction flagged in-place)

### What Non actually did (factual)

Walked into a room of TKC employees (mixed roles — engineers, PMs, HR, maybe managers). The host introduced him as someone who works differently from their usual bosses.

Started by asking: *"Who thinks their work is boring?"* Hands went up. Immediately diagnosed the cause: not the projects themselves, but the way of working.

**Introduced the 4C framework (Compensation, Cause, Community, Career) — on the spot, not from a slide.** Asked which C was missing. They answered. He moved on.

Then showed a **gamified HR dashboard (Dragon Quest style).** Explained that in the future, employees would be hero cards with skills scores, not Excel rows. Project leaders would set required skill percentages, and the system would suggest optimal teams. He joked about the system detecting office affairs. He also mentioned that if HR decided to fire someone, the system would generate an AI-written recommendation letter to help them find a new job — which landed as both funny and unsettling.

Then introduced the **Ninja Squad concept:** ninjas kill with a pencil, not expensive weapons. Minimal resources, maximum results.

Next, presented a real government challenge: a **national digital TV platform with a budget of 20 million baht** — but 20 million would normally only pay for a feasibility study, not the actual system. Gave the teams 5 minutes to brainstorm how to build it with that budget.

After they presented their ideas (pilots, mobile-first, lobbying, etc.), he revealed his **working prototype — built in 45 minutes, zero cost, running on an old Android phone with 21 browser tabs**. Demonstrated live channels, previews, reminders, and an emergency alert function that could push messages to 70 million people for free. Also showed a full feasibility study with ROI calculations.

Then gave a second challenge: *if the boss gave you no money and one week to win the project, what would you do?* Teams gave answers. Non countered with the ninja approach: build it first, show it, then write the TOR around it.

Cycled through other demos: **Phuket governor's war room** (satellite layers, accident hotspots, real-time news), **Chulalongkorn University energy monitoring**, a **Dao De Jing teaching platform built for a grieving friend.**

Finally, drew the **S-curve** — slow growth, rapid growth, plateau, decline. Told them that TKC's core business has been stagnant for two years, that last year's profit came from investments, not operations, and that if they don't create a new S-curve, **layoffs will come within two years**. Set a **one-month mission: each team must produce one working prototype.** He would provide API credits and coaching.

Closed with: *"Let's do this."*
